NSSAIs

Posted on 2017-08-06

NSSAI is a collection of S-NSSAI. Each S-NSSAI points to a NSI. Two different S-NSSAIs may point to the same NSI.
S-NSSAI = SST + SD. SST may be standardized values such as eMMB, URLLC or MIoT. It could also be PLMN specific values and in that case the S-NSSAI is associated with a specific PLMN and can only be used in that PLMN.

In UDM the UE’s subscription data may contain Subscribed NSSAI and some of them (up to 8 S-NSSAIs) might be marked as Default S-NSSAIs.
At UE side it may be configured by the operator some Configured NSSAI. It may also store the Allowed NSSAI received from the Registration procedure.
The AMF UE context may also store the Allowed NSSAI for the UE.
The UE forms the requested NSSAI from the configured NSSAI and/or stored allowed NSSAI. If none of those NSSAIs is available, the UE doesn’t include any requested NSSAI in its message.
